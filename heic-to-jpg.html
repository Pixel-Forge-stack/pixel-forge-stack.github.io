What Is HEIC

What Is HEIC and Why Do iPhones Use It?

HEIC stands for High Efficiency Image Container. It is the default photo format used by iPhones running iOS 11 and later. Apple introduced HEIC because it produces smaller file sizes than JPG while maintaining the same or better image quality. A HEIC photo is typically about half the size of the same photo in JPG format, which means your iPhone can store twice as many photos in the same amount of storage space.

While HEIC is great for storing photos on your iPhone, it creates a problem when you try to share those photos with people who are not using Apple devices. Windows PCs, Android phones, and many websites and applications do not support HEIC natively. If you have ever sent an iPhone photo to a friend or uploaded it to a website and it did not display correctly, this is likely the reason.

Converting HEIC to JPG solves this problem instantly. JPG is the most universally supported image format in the world. Every device, every browser, every application, and every website supports JPG. Once you convert your HEIC photo to JPG, you can share it, upload it, print it, or use it anywhere without any compatibility issues.

Our HEIC to JPG converter works directly in your browser. You upload the HEIC file, the browser reads it, and the tool converts it to JPG and downloads it to your device. The conversion is instant and completely free.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Why can't I open HEIC files on my Windows PC? ▾
Windows does not support HEIC natively. You can either install the HEIF Image Extensions from the Microsoft Store, or simply convert your HEIC files to JPG using our tool, which is the easier and faster option.
Does converting HEIC to JPG reduce quality? ▾
There is a very small quality reduction when converting from HEIC to JPG because JPG is a lossy format. However, at high quality settings the difference is not visible to the naked eye. Our tool uses 92% quality for the JPG output, which preserves excellent visual quality.
How do I stop my iPhone from taking photos in HEIC? ▾
Go to Settings on your iPhone, then Camera, then Formats. Change the setting from "High Efficiency" to "Most Compatible". This will make your iPhone save photos as JPG instead of HEIC going forward.
Is this converter free and private? ▾
Yes, completely free and completely private. Your photos are never uploaded to any server. The conversion happens inside your browser using the browser's built-in image handling capabilities. No one else can see your photos at any point.
