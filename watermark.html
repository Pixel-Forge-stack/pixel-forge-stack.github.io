Why Watermark

Why Should You Add a Watermark to Your Images?

A watermark is a visible text or logo placed on top of an image to identify who created it and to discourage unauthorised use. If you are a photographer, designer, artist, or content creator, watermarking your work is one of the most important things you can do to protect it online.

When you share images online without a watermark, it is very easy for others to download and use them without giving you credit or without your permission. A watermark makes it clear that the image belongs to you. Even if someone crops or edits the image slightly, a well-placed watermark remains visible and identifies you as the creator.

Watermarks are also commonly used by businesses on their product photos to prevent competitors from copying them. They are used by real estate agents on property photos, by stock photo sites to protect their library, and by wedding photographers who want to share preview images with clients before delivery.

With Pixel Forge Stack, adding a watermark is completely free, takes just a few seconds, and gives you full control over the text, position, size, color, and opacity. You can make the watermark subtle enough that it does not distract from the image, or bold enough that it is impossible to miss.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Pixel Forge Stack add its own watermark to my image? ▾
Absolutely not. We never add any watermarks, logos, or branding to your images. The only watermark on your downloaded image will be the one you create yourself. This is true for all our tools.
Is my image uploaded to a server? ▾
No. Your image never leaves your device. The watermark is applied using the HTML5 Canvas API directly in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server at any point.
Can I add a logo as a watermark instead of text? ▾
Currently this tool supports text watermarks. You can customise the font, size, color, position, and opacity to create a watermark that looks professional. Image logo watermarks are on our roadmap for a future update.
Can I add watermark to multiple images at once? ▾
Currently the tool processes one image at a time. Simply download the watermarked image and upload the next one. Batch processing is something we are working on for future updates.
