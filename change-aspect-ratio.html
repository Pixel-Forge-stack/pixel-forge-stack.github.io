Understanding Aspect Ratios

What Is an Aspect Ratio and Why Does It Matter?

An aspect ratio is the proportional relationship between the width and height of an image. It is written as two numbers separated by a colon, such as 16:9 or 1:1. The first number represents the width and the second represents the height. So a 16:9 image is 16 units wide for every 9 units tall — which is the shape of a widescreen TV or YouTube video.

Aspect ratio matters because different platforms, devices, and use cases have specific requirements. If you upload an image with the wrong aspect ratio to a platform, it will either be cropped automatically — often cutting out important parts of your image — or it will display with black bars on the sides, which looks unprofessional. Getting the aspect ratio right before uploading saves you time and makes your images look their best everywhere.

Fill Modes Explained

What Do the Fill Modes Mean?

When you change the aspect ratio of an image, the new canvas shape may not perfectly match the original image. For example, if you have a landscape photo and you want to make it a square, there will be extra space above and below the image. The fill mode controls what goes in that extra space.

Blur Fill takes the image itself and creates a blurred, zoomed-in version of it to fill the empty areas. This looks the most natural and professional because the background matches the colours and tones of the original image. This is the fill mode used by Instagram when you choose not to crop a photo.

Black Bars fills the extra space with solid black, like a letterbox effect. This is common in cinema and gives a professional, cinematic look. It works especially well for dark or moody images.

White Bars fills the extra space with solid white. This is clean and minimal, and works well for product photos, blog images, and anything that will sit on a white background.

Crop to Fit does not add any fill at all. Instead, it crops the image to exactly match the new aspect ratio, keeping the centre of the image and removing the edges. This is the best option when you do not want any bars or blur and the main subject of your image is in the centre.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Does changing the aspect ratio reduce image quality? ▾
No. Changing the aspect ratio does not reduce the quality of the image itself. The fill areas (blur, black, or white bars) are added around the original image, which keeps its full resolution and quality. If you use Crop to Fit, the image is cropped but the remaining part keeps its original quality.
What is the best aspect ratio for Instagram? ▾
For Instagram posts, the best ratios are 1:1 (square), 4:5 (portrait), and 1.91:1 (landscape). For Instagram Stories and Reels, use 9:16. Our tool supports all of these. For the widest reach, many creators use 4:5 because it takes up more screen space in the feed.
Can I set a completely custom aspect ratio? ▾
Yes. Use the Custom Width and Custom Height fields to enter any ratio you need. For example, entering 7 and 3 will create a 7:3 ratio. You can enter any two positive numbers.
Is this tool free and private? ▾
Yes, completely free and completely private. All processing happens in your browser. Your image is never uploaded to any server. No account or signup is needed.
