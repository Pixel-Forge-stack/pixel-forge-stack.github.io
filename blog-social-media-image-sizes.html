The Right Image Size for Instagram, Facebook & YouTube (2026)

Upload at the wrong size and platforms crop or compress your image for you. Here's how to get it right the first time.

Every social platform displays images at a specific size. Upload something that doesn't match, and the platform will crop, stretch, or recompress it — which is exactly how you end up with an off-centre profile photo or a blurry cover image.

The fix is simple: resize your image to the platform's exact recommended dimensions before you upload it. Here's a quick reference for the sizes that matter most right now.

Instagram

PlacementSizeRatio
Square feed post1080 × 1080px1:1
Portrait feed post1080 × 1350px4:5
Story / Reel1080 × 1920px9:16
Profile picture320 × 320px1:1

The square 1080 × 1080px format remains the safest default for feed posts — it displays consistently across profile grids and search results.

YouTube thumbnails must also stay under a 2MB file size — using around 85-92% quality after resizing to 1280 × 720px is usually well within that limit.

A Simple Rule of Thumb

When in doubt, resize to the exact recommended pixel dimensions rather than "close enough." Platforms rarely upscale an undersized image gracefully, and downsizing an oversized one can shift the crop away from what you intended.

If you're preparing images for several platforms at once, it's worth resizing a separate copy for each one rather than reusing a single size everywhere.
