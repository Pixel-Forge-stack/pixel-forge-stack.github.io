Print Size Reference

Common Print Sizes and Their Pixel Dimensions

When preparing images for printing, the most important thing to understand is the relationship between physical size (in inches or millimetres), resolution (in DPI), and pixel dimensions. DPI stands for Dots Per Inch — it tells the printer how many dots of ink to place in each inch of the printed output. The higher the DPI, the sharper and more detailed the print will be.

The formula is simple: Pixel Width = Physical Width (in inches) × DPI. So a 4×6 inch photo at 300 DPI needs to be 1200×1800 pixels. A passport photo that is 35×45 millimetres at 300 DPI needs to be 413×531 pixels. Our tool calculates all of this for you automatically — just choose your print size and download.

Understanding DPI

What DPI Should You Use for Printing?

DPI stands for Dots Per Inch. It is the measure of how many dots of ink a printer will place in each linear inch of the printed image. The higher the DPI, the more dots, and the sharper and more detailed the final print will look.

300 DPI is the standard for high-quality photo printing. This is what professional photo labs, printing services, and office printers use. If you are printing a photo to hang on a wall, put in a frame, or give to someone, use 300 DPI.

150 DPI is acceptable for draft prints or images that will be viewed from a distance, such as posters or signs. The print will look fine when viewed normally but may show pixelation if you look very closely.

72 DPI is the standard resolution for screens, not for print. Images prepared at 72 DPI will look blurry and pixelated when printed at any significant size. Only use 72 DPI if the image is going to be displayed on a screen only.

600 DPI is used for professional printing of very detailed graphics, fine art, and high-end commercial work. Most home printers and standard photo labs print at 300 DPI, so 600 DPI images will not look noticeably better unless you are using a professional printing service.

For most everyday use cases — printing family photos, passport photos, or documents — 300 DPI is the right choice. Our tool defaults to 300 DPI for this reason.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What DPI should I use for photo printing? ▾
For standard photo printing, 300 DPI is the recommended setting. This is the standard used by photo labs, printing shops, and most home printers. It produces sharp, high-quality prints that look great both up close and at normal viewing distance.
What is the pixel size for a passport photo? ▾
A standard passport photo is 35mm × 45mm. At 300 DPI, this works out to approximately 413 × 531 pixels. However, different countries have different passport photo requirements — always check the specific requirements for the country you are applying to, as some require different sizes or specific background colours.
My photo looks blurry when I print it — why? ▾
This usually happens because the original image does not have enough pixels for the print size you have chosen. For example, if you have a 400×300 pixel image and you try to print it at A4 size (which needs 2480×3508 pixels at 300 DPI), the image will be very blurry because the printer has to stretch the small image to fill a much larger space. Always start with a high-resolution original image and then resize it for print.
