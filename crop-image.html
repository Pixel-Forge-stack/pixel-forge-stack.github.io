The Basics

What Does It Mean to Crop an Image?

Cropping means removing the outer parts of an image to focus on what matters. Think of it like taking a pair of scissors to a printed photo and cutting away the edges you do not want. When you crop a digital image, you are selecting a rectangular area to keep and everything outside that area gets removed.

Cropping is one of the most powerful and commonly used tools in photography and design. A good crop can completely change the feel of a photo. It can remove distracting backgrounds, bring the subject closer, change the shape of the image, or make it fit perfectly in a specific space like an Instagram post or a website banner.

With Pixel Forge Stack, cropping is done entirely inside your browser. Your image is loaded onto a canvas on your screen, you select the area you want to keep, and the tool cuts it for you. The entire process happens on your own device, which means your image is completely private and never gets sent to any server.

Ratios Explained

What Are Aspect Ratios and Which One Should You Use?

An aspect ratio is simply the relationship between the width and height of an image. For example, a 1:1 ratio means the image is a perfect square — the width and height are equal. A 16:9 ratio means the image is much wider than it is tall, like a widescreen TV or YouTube video.

Choosing the right aspect ratio depends on where you are going to use the image. Here is a quick guide to help you choose:

Free (Custom)

Vertical ratio. Perfect for Instagram Stories, TikTok videos, and mobile-first content.

Standard DSLR camera ratio. Good for printing 6×4 inch photos and photo albums.

2:3

Portrait version of 3:2. Good for portrait photos and Pinterest images.

5:4

Used for 8×10 inch prints and some older monitor sizes. Good for print photography.

Pro Tips

Tips for Cropping Images Like a Pro

Here are some useful tips that will help you get the best results when cropping your images:

Use the rule of thirds. When cropping a photo, imagine the image divided into nine equal boxes by two horizontal and two vertical lines. Try to place the main subject at one of the four points where those lines intersect. This creates a more visually interesting composition than simply centring the subject.

Do not crop too tight around the subject. Leaving a little bit of space around the edges makes the image feel more natural and comfortable to look at. Cropping too close can make the image feel cramped or cut off.

Think about where the image will be used before cropping. If you are cropping a photo for Instagram, use 1:1 for posts or 9:16 for stories. If it is for a YouTube thumbnail, use 16:9. Cropping to the right ratio from the start saves you time later.

Use manual coordinates for pixel-perfect crops. If you need to crop to exact pixel dimensions, use the X, Y, Width, and Height input fields. This is especially useful for passport photos or product images that require very specific sizes.

Keep the original image. Our tool processes everything in your browser and does not modify your original file, but it is always a good habit to keep a backup of the original high-resolution image before cropping. Once you crop and save, you cannot get back the removed parts from the downloaded file.

Save as PNG if quality is critical. If you need the highest possible quality for your cropped image, choose PNG as the output format. PNG is lossless, meaning no quality is lost during saving. This is especially important for logos, text images, or anything where sharpness is critical.

Is the crop tool really free? ▾
Yes, completely free. There is no paid version, no subscription, and no limit on how many images you can crop. All features are available to everyone at no cost.
Does cropping reduce the image quality? ▾
Cropping itself does not reduce image quality. When you crop an image, you are simply removing pixels from the edges. The pixels that remain are exactly the same as in the original image. However, if you save as JPG, there is a very small amount of compression applied. To avoid any quality loss at all, save your cropped image as PNG.
Can I crop a photo to a circle or oval shape? ▾
Our current tool crops images to rectangular shapes. For a circular crop, we suggest cropping to a square first using the 1:1 ratio, then using CSS border-radius in your code or design to display it as a circle. We are working on adding a circular crop option in a future update.

No. Your image is never uploaded anywhere. The entire cropping process happens inside your browser on your own device using JavaScript. This makes our tool 100% private and secure. No one else can see or access your image.

What file formats can I upload and download? ▾
