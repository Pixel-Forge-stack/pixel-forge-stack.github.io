YouTube Thumbnail Guide

What Is the Perfect YouTube Thumbnail Size?

The official recommended YouTube thumbnail size is 1280 × 720 pixels with an aspect ratio of 16:9. This is the size YouTube recommends for all custom thumbnails, and it is the size our tool outputs automatically. YouTube also requires that the thumbnail file size be under 2MB, and it should be in JPG, PNG, GIF, or BMP format.

Best Practices

Tips for Creating Great YouTube Thumbnails

Use high contrast and bright colours. Thumbnails are small when viewed in YouTube search results and on mobile. Bold, contrasting colours help your thumbnail stand out against the grey and white background of YouTube.

Include a human face if possible. Research consistently shows that thumbnails with human faces, especially with strong expressions, get higher click-through rates. People are naturally drawn to faces.

Keep text short and large. If you add text to your thumbnail, keep it to three to five words maximum and make it large enough to read on a small screen. Viewers often browse YouTube on their phones.

Make sure your thumbnail is under 2MB. YouTube requires thumbnails to be under 2MB. After resizing with our tool, if your file is still large, use our compress tool to reduce the file size without losing visible quality.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the YouTube thumbnail size? ▾
YouTube recommends 1280 × 720 pixels at a 16:9 aspect ratio. This is what our tool outputs automatically. The maximum file size YouTube allows for thumbnails is 2MB.
What format should my YouTube thumbnail be? ▾
YouTube accepts JPG, PNG, GIF, and BMP formats for thumbnails. JPG is the most common choice as it produces the smallest file size while still looking great. Our tool downloads the thumbnail as JPG by default.
What does Fit, Fill, and Cover mean? ▾
Fit scales the image so it fits entirely within 1280×720 without cropping, adding black bars if the aspect ratio does not match. Fill stretches the image to exactly 1280×720, which may distort it. Cover scales the image so it fills the entire 1280×720 area and crops the excess — this usually looks the most natural.
