About This Tool

Why Do You Need to Rotate or Flip an Image?

There are many situations in everyday life where you might need to rotate or flip an image. The most common one is when a photo was taken with your phone held sideways and it saves in the wrong orientation. You open the photo on your computer and it appears rotated 90 degrees. Our rotate tool fixes this in one click.

Flipping an image is also a very common need. When you take a selfie with your phone's front camera, the image is often saved as a mirror image of what you saw. Flipping it horizontally corrects this and shows you as others see you. Flipping is also widely used in design, where a mirrored version of an element creates a balanced and symmetrical layout.

Other common reasons to rotate or flip include correcting scanned documents that came out sideways, preparing images for use in designs where they need to face a specific direction, and fixing photos taken by cameras with incorrect orientation metadata.

With Pixel Forge Stack, all of this is just one click away. Upload your image, click the rotation or flip you need, and download the corrected image instantly.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Does rotating an image reduce quality? ▾
Rotating by exactly 90, 180, or 270 degrees does not reduce image quality at all because these rotations simply rearrange the pixels without any interpolation. If you save as PNG (lossless), there is absolutely no quality loss. Saving as JPG applies a small amount of compression, but at the default quality setting this is barely noticeable.
What is the difference between rotate and flip? ▾
Rotating turns the image around its centre point by a specific number of degrees. Flipping creates a mirror image. Flipping horizontally creates a left-right mirror. Flipping vertically creates an upside-down mirror. You can also combine them — for example, rotate 90 degrees and then flip horizontally — to achieve any orientation you need.
Is my image uploaded to a server? ▾
No. Everything happens inside your browser. Your image is never sent to any server. The rotation and flip transformations are applied using the HTML5 Canvas API directly on your device, making the process completely private.
Can I apply multiple transformations? ▾
Yes. You can click multiple buttons in sequence. For example, you can rotate 90 degrees to the right, then flip horizontally. Each click applies the transformation to the current state of the image. The preview updates in real time so you can see the result immediately.
